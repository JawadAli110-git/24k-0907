SI UNITS

What are SI Units?

The International System of Units (SI) is the globally accepted standard for measurement. It provides a consistent framework for measuring various physical quantities, ensuring that measurements are uniform and comparable worldwide. The SI system is based on seven fundamental units, each representing a basic physical quantity:

SI BASE UNITS
Base Quantity Symbol Base Unit Symbol
Time t Second s
Length L Meter m
Mass M Kilogram kg
Electric Current I Ampere A
Thermodynamic Temperature T Kelvin K
Amount of Substance n Mole mol
Luminous Intensity Iv Candela cd

Importance

The International System of Units (SI) is vital because it provides a universal standard for measurements, ensuring consistency and accuracy globally. Its base-10 structure simplifies conversions, and its precision aids scientific and technological advancements. Additionally, SI units facilitate effective communication and collaboration among scientists and engineers worldwide.
